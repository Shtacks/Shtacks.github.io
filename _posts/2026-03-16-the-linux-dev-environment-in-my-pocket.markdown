## Is Mobile Development Practical?

The answer to this question has always been a hard "no" from me. It has never made sense to even try to develop apps on a phone, or even scripts for that matter. Until I recently pivoted from using LLMs through their native applications or agentic extensions in VS Code to using the CLI tools, I haven't ever wondered how much I could squeeze out of my phone. After seeing OpenAI's Codex CLI in action on my PC, I got the idea to try and see if truly agentic AI was possible on a mobile device.
## Termux

I did a **very small** amount of research and found that Android had an incredible tool available: Termux. Linux terminal on my phone. Once I started messing with this, my mind was blown. First thing I did was figure out how to get Codex CLI running, and sure enough, it was possible. The possibilities that had opened themselves up to me at this point were incredible. Git, Python, Agentic AI; all at my fingertips. 

## My New Daily Workflow

With all this newfound power, I quickly realized how much I could actually accomplish while on the go, or simply just while away from my desk. I'm still very early on in my exploration of what works well on mobile as far as development, but already I have a nice flow where I can use git to bounce from my phone to my PC on a python project. 

Not to mention, this whole website I built on my phone using Codex, Termux, and Jekyll while rocking my 3 week old son to sleep in a rocking chair. How incredible is that?
